Machine Learning

The dawn of AI

Posted by Khai

What does it do?

Machine learning is a subfield of AI, in which the machine makes a prediction or product a result based on past data or experiences. Machine learning algorithms use statistics to find common patterns in a massive amount of data and use that pattern to make an accurate prediction. Furthermore, if the information can be stored digitally, it can be fed to the machine-learning algorithm.

There are a few techniques that machine learning used:

  1. Supervised learning
  2. Unsupervised learning
  3. Semi-supervised learning
  4. Reinforcement learning

Supervised learning

In this approach, the algorithm is trained by given labeled inputs and expected outputs, then the algorithm will try to figure out the pattern (relationship) between the two data. This technique is divided into main types: Classification and Recession. The classification algorithm focuses on discrete output, where the main goal is to predict the output as accurately as possible. The recession algorithm focuses on continuous data, where the main goal is to predict the output as close to the actual output as possible.

Unsupervised learning

In this approach, the algorithm is given unlabeled inputs and no output. The algorithm will then try to figure out the hidden pattern between the data. Furthermore, unsupervised learning is grouped into two types: Clustering and Association. In the clustering algorithm, it will find a pattern or a structure in a collection of unlabeled data. In the association algorithm, it will find the relationship between two (or more) data object inside a large database.

Semi-supervised learning

This approach is a hybrid between unsupervised and supervised learning, where the algorithm will take in partially labeled data and determine the relationship between those data. Because of that, this technique can be used where there is a lack of quality data. Moreover, this technique has yielded accurate results and is more suitable for many real-world problems.

Reinforcement learning

this is the latest approach in machine learning, where the algorithm will learn from trial and errors to achieve its goal. The algorithm will try to do different things and are rewarded (or rated) based on whether the action helps to reach its goal.

The field is constantly expanding its scope as it is responsible for the majority of AI advancement and application. Because of that, it is used to develop many data-driven applications, where humans cannot process. For example, the recommendation systems on YouTube and Netflix; search engines like Google and Bing; advertisement feeds on Facebook and Instagram. In all of the examples above, each platform will collect as much user data as possible (where you are from, what kind of video do you watch, what you recently search for) and feed those data into a machine-learning algorithm to produce a prediction on what you might want next.

What is the likely impact?

Until now, machine learning and AI have already integrated into our daily lives. It is in the search recommendation, voice/facial recognition, phone support (like Siri), etc. However, one aspect of AI that potentially has a high impact positively and negatively is its automation capability. Moreover, like the industrial revolution, AI will replace and create a lot of jobs. For example, we could see this impact clearly in the manufacturing industry, where many American companies have been using robots more and more. These changes cause an increase in production with fewer resources for the company. However, it also causes a lot of people to lose their current job or force them to change jobs. Many lucky people would be able to make the transition, but some will not, like an older worker who will not be as adaptive. On the order hand, for people who can make the transition, they would have a more well-paid job. Another example is the self-driving car since the AI is not likely to make a mistake or fell asleep, it will reduce the number of accidents on the road when this technology is fully developed. The jobs that AI will be replacing will be a redundant job or a dangerous job where we do not want any error. On that note, to balance that, AI would also create a lot of new jobs. The machine would still need maintenance, so the company would be more likely to retrain their workers to do so. Moreover, since the machine still needs to be trained to do the task, a job like data pruning for training would be created.

How will this affect you

So, because of the fourth industrial revolution of AI, many people would have to adapt to it, myself included. For starter, I have already been using AI in my daily lives, like Google map, search engine, grammar checking engine, etc. So, for my generation, we have already grown up with all these technologies and expected it to evolve in the future. Moreover, because I am an IT student, I have to be able to adapt to different technology and languages almost every new semester. Thanks to that, I would be better prepared for any changes that the AI revolution would cause to the industry and be able to stay ahead of the trend and used the rising advancement of AI as an advantage to boost my career. On that note, in contrast to most science fiction films, AI would be considerably beneficial for humans. As you saw in some of the examples above, AI and machine learning could speed up our technology development significantly, improve our mortality, and could potentially help reverse the effect of climate change. For example, my parent generation would benefit greatly from the medical and technology advancement when they retired. However, for most people who do not have any experience with these technologies, it would be very challenging for them to adapt. Those are the poor, the people who do not have access to the internet (around 50% world population) or the people who have low adaptability. Because of that, in the future, we might see an increase in development toward accessibility for everyone.
