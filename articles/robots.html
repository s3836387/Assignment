Robots are often understood to mean mechanics and software come together and are made to do everything automatically and controlled by humans. Contemporarily, everyone wants to do everything automatically because it helps companies or organizations reduce their number of employees. It is beneficial to many benefits that scientists are investing in to create robots that can benefit society and for science. Scientists have gained some great achievements in the brightness of the robots to serve humans. She is one of the robots with vivid facial expressions and makes people when talking to her seem like communicating with a real person. Self-driving cars are being tested by Google many times and will soon be launched with self-driving cars. Currently, self-driving cars can drive up to 1000km without human intervention or impact. The CEO of Uber has a very disruptive idea when he wants to replace all drivers with smart cars. Cashiers are also the job of being replaced by robots and automatic checkout robots that are present at many grocery stores around the world. It will help people to use their limbs less for labor. The economy is playing a huge role in robot development so when we want to use robots, we need to invest in technology as well as software development to make it the most complete. It needs to be tried many times before it can be put on the market for the consumer because when we try it, it is easy to spot the error and be able to fix it quickly.

Autonomous vehicles today:

Billions of dollars invested in autonomous vehicle technology, which has helped people make spectacular strides in the technology age but it also reminds us of how much longer we are going to complete it. Currently, people still do not fully trust autonomous vehicles because no company has yet allowed a fully self-driving ride on all roads without a supervisor. The autonomous vehicle industry split into two camps: the companies that have created very limited technologies for public users ( complex cruise control systems ) and some companies have introduced fully automated technology but under limited circumstance.
